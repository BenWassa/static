Executive Dysfunction & Inertia

Identical behaviors like task avoidance stem from radically different neurobiological tunnels. Evaluating the “Inertia Signature” uncovers the true driver.

The Monotropic Split

Autism

Intense difficulty initiating or shifting tasks because recalibrating focus demands a tangible bodily reset.

Analysis

Reduce transition costs with sensory dampening before requesting switching.

Interest-Based System

ADHD

The PFC cannot inhibit irrelevant stimuli; tasks devoid of dopamine-triggering interest feel threatening and induce freeze.

Analysis

Scaffold with novelty or motivational hooks to recruit executive circuits.

Functional Freeze

C-PTSD

Chronic hypervigilance keeps the PFC offline—cognitive energy is consumed by scanning for threat.

Analysis

Prioritize safety, down-regulate the HPA axis, and avoid demand until autonomic flexibility returns.

Social Withdrawal Dynamics

Social retreat often signals survival rather than pathology. Discerning whether it is driven by burnout, rejection sensitivity, or hypervigilance shapes intervention.

Autistic Burnout

Driver: Absolute energy depletion from prolonged masking.

Trigger: Chronic social or sensory demand.

State: Shutdown · Motor/cognitive slowing

ADHD · Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria

Driver: Perceived criticism or social failure.

Trigger: Threat to identity; all-or-nothing thinking.

State: Emotional crash; chest or gut pain

Trauma (C-PTSD)

Driver: Hypervigilance and mistrust.

Trigger: Cues signaling interpersonal harm.

State: Sympathetic takeover; high arousal

Social Anxiety

Driver: Fear of negative evaluation.

Trigger: Social scrutiny or performance pressure.

State: Chronic anticipatory fear; avoidance

Sensory Overload Signatures

Intense World Theory

Everyday inputs can feel threatening when filtering failures overload the nervous system. The shape of that overload varies by condition.

  • Autistic “Bottom-Up” Hypersensitivity: Reduced thalamic GABA tone makes background noise intolerable.
  • ADHD “Top-Down” Gating Failure: Aberrant LC-NE gain causes all inputs to compete equally for attention.
  • C-PTSD Threat-Prioritized Processing: The HPA axis stays on high alert scanning for danger, consuming cognitive bandwidth.

Shutdown vs. Dissociation

Although both look like withdrawal, shutdowns and dissociation reveal different neural dynamics.

Feature Autistic Shutdown C-PTSD Dissociation
Primary Trigger Sensory or cognitive overstimulation Emotional trauma or psychological pain
Level of Awareness Grounded; aware but unable to engage Detached; “watching from outside”
Body Experience Hypersensitivity; still/frozen Analgesia; numb or disconnected
Memory of Event Intact; loss of verbal processing Patchy; “lost time”
Immediate Support Low sensory input; solitude Grounding; emotional safety

The Load Capacity Framework

The Reservoir Model

Effective capacity (Ceff) is dynamic. Recovery is non-linear, so it takes longer to replenish than to deplete.

  • Fast Fatigue: Immediate depletion (minutes) requiring prompt withdrawal.
  • Slow Allostatic Load: Cumulative wear-and-tear (months) that lowers baseline tolerance.

Calc:

Ceff = C0 - (F · A)

Where C0 is baseline capacity, F fast fatigue, and A accumulative load.

Metric 1

AASPIRE Burnout measure differentiates burnout from depression.

Metric 2

Switching costs quantify cognitive recalibration constraints.

Metric 3

Heart rate variability and cortisol track autonomic flexibility.

Clinical Synthesis & Intervention

Sensory/Cognitive Overload (Autism)

Low-pass filtering failure. Strategy: Adjust the environment, regulate sensory input, and reduce manager demands. Outcome: Decreased afferent drive and sympathetic activation.

Dopaminergic/Attentional Failure (ADHD)

PFC instability. Strategy: Interest-based structure and explicit executive scaffolding. Outcome: Stabilized amygdala-PFC axis; reengaged cortical control.

Trauma-Induced Hyperarousal (C-PTSD)

HPA-axis dysregulation. Strategy: Trauma-informed therapy and autonomic regulation. Outcome: Restored flexibility and downshifted stress axis.

Evaluative Worry (Anxiety)

Amygdalar hyperactivity. Strategy: Cognitive techniques to challenge fear patterns. Outcome: Reduced amygdalar activation and improved regulation.
